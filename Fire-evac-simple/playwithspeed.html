Welcome to the last tutorial of the page! In this tutorial, you will be learning how to vary the speeds of the minions by varying the minimum speed and maximum speed that each minion can possibly take.

In the last tutorial, you explored the effect of pre-movement time on the overall evacuation time. Now, we will investigate the effect of movement speeds during evacuation on the total evacuation time. This shall be done through varying the sliders "min-my-speed" and "max-my-speed".

Minimum and Maximum Speed

The speed of each minion is determined by a random value between "min-my-speed" and "max-my-speed". A value of 5 means that the minion is moving at a speed of 1 patch per second, which translates to 0.35 metres per second. An average human walks at 1.4 metres per second, so we will leave you to do the mathematics! If you want the speed of the minions to be all the same during movement, set "min-my-speed" = "max-my-speed", and you will have the speed you desire. Due to the conversion, we suggest that you lower the speed of the simulation (through varying the speed slider at the top of the page) so as to see the minions moving, or else they would be to too fast for the naked eye to observe.

Take your time to explore the effect of changing the minimum and maximum speeds on the evacuation time. A gentle reminder to vary "number-of-minions" to see more significant effects of the changes in speed!
